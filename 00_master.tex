\begin{abstract} %
  
Housing is a key social determinant of health and well-being. However, relatively little is known about whether home owners live longer than their renter counterparts. Using large-scale linked administrative data, we produce the first US-based estimates of the association between home-ownership and longevity. We then use a sibling-based identification strategy to estimate the causal effect of home ownership on longevity in the first part of the twentieth century, finding owning a home has an effect of over half a year on longevity. 
 
% \vspace{10pt}

 \end{abstract}
